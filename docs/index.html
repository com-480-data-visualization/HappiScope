Understanding Global Happiness

The World Happiness Report ranks countries based on citizens' self-reported happiness levels and analyzes various factors contributing to well-being. Explore key insights from a decade of happiness data:

Top Countries

Nordic countries consistently dominate the top positions, with Finland maintaining the highest happiness ranking since 2018.

Trends

Most countries show remarkable stability in happiness, with some notable exceptions showing significant improvement or decline.

Regional Patterns

Clear regional clusters emerge with Northern and Western European countries typically ranking higher in happiness.

Global Happiness Map

Explore happiness scores across the world. The map allows you to compare countries and regions, and see how happiness is distributed globally.

Happiness Over Time

Track how happiness has evolved in different countries over the past decade. Compare trajectories and identify significant changes.

Key Insights

  • Switzerland peaked in happiness in 2016 and has gradually declined while maintaining high global standing
  • China shows steady improvement from 5.2 in 2015 to nearly 6.0 in 2024
  • The United States displays more variability with a notable drop in 2024

About the Project

Methodology

HappiScope visualizes data from the World Happiness Report (2015-2024), which ranks countries based on citizens' self-reported happiness levels. The data is collected through the Gallup World Poll, using nationally representative surveys in approximately 150 countries with samples of about 1,000 individuals per country.

Additional data sources include the Human Development Index (HDI) from the UN Development Programme and population data from the World Population Prospects.

Data Sources

  • World Happiness Report Official Website - Annual reports from 2012-2024
  • Human Development Index (HDI) - Health, education, and income metrics
  • World Population Prospects (UN) - Demographic data
